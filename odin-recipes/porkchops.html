Pork Chops

Description

Easy Chinese style pork chops that pair great with rice and some greens for a full meal.

These pork chops are easy to make and can be prepped well in advance for a quick and easy meal.

Ingredients

  • 3 tbsp of neutral oil
  • 2 pork chops, thawed
  • 1/2 tbsp Shaoxing wine -- or any Chinese cooking wine
  • 1 tbsp soy sauce
  • 1 tbsp potato starch
  • 1/2 tsp salt
  • 1/2 cup water

Steps

  1. Score and pound pork chops so they're about 1/4 inch thick at most.
  2. In a bag or a bowl, combine soy sauce, salt, and wine to create a light marinade.
    Add pork chops to the marinade and leave for at least 10 minutes to let the flavors meld.
  3. When you're ready to cook the pork chops, combine a little bit of the water, just a few tablesppons
    to the potato starch in a small bowl to make a potato starch slurry. Mix thoroughly to ensure no clumps.
    Add the potato starch slurry to the bag or bowl with the pork chops and mix to cover the porkchops.
  4. In a pan, add oil and heat on high. Make sure to swirl the oil around to coat the bottom of the pan.
    Test the oil by sticking a chopstick or other wooden utensil in the oil.
    If you see bubbles around the utensil, the oil is ready and you can carefully drop in your porkchops.
  5. Cook the pork chops on one side for about 30 seconds each, until each side is lightly colored.
  6. Add the remaining water to the marinade and pour into the pan. Cook at medium high heat for about 5 minutes.
    Make sure to flip the pork chops half way through.
  7. Remove from the heat and plate and pair with rice and veggies for a complete meal.
